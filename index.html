Philosophy

Predictability over generality.

Most systems don't need unbounded queues, dynamic allocation, or multi-producer flexibility. They need bounded, specialized primitives that do one thing well and never surprise you at runtime.

01

SPSC over MPMC

One producer, one consumer? Don't pay for synchronization you don't need. The constraint is the performance.

02

Pre-allocation

Allocate at startup, never on the hot path. You pay the cost once, not when processing the millionth message.

03

Specialization

A conflation slot isn't a queue of size 1. It's a different thing entirely. Different problems deserve different solutions.

Why Nexus

Built for systems where microseconds matter.

No surprises on the hot path

Every crate documents its constraints. No hidden allocations, no unbounded growth, no syscalls where you don't expect them.

Consistent tail latency

Optimized for p99 / p999 and jitter under real load, measured in cycles with cores pinned, not gamed in synthetic microbenchmarks.

Compose what you need

Small, focused crates with minimal dependency trees. Take the conflation slot without the whole runtime. Honest constraints, no kitchen sinks.
