Philosophy

Predictability over generality.

Most systems don't need unbounded queues, dynamic allocation, or multi-producer flexibility. They need bounded, specialized primitives that do one thing well and never surprise you at runtime. Every crate is small, focused, and honest about its constraints — because the constraint is where the performance comes from.

01

SPSC over MPMC

One producer, one consumer? Don't pay for synchronization you don't need. The constraint is the performance.

02

Pre-allocation

Allocate at startup, never on the hot path. You pay the cost once, not when processing the millionth message.

03

Bounded over unbounded

Know your capacity. Reject rather than allocate. Unbounded growth is a design smell that hides latency cliffs.

04

Specialization

A conflation slot isn't a queue of size 1. It's a different thing entirely. Different problems deserve different solutions.

05

Single writer

One owner of mutable state, no contention. Coordination you can reason about beats cleverness you can't.

06

Honest constraints

SPSC means SPSC. Every crate documents exactly what it guarantees and what it doesn't — no sneaking in extra producers.

Performance, proven

Safe abstractions, optimal codegen — nothing in between.

Every hot path is safe by default and audited down to the instruction. The safe abstraction compiles to the same code you'd write by hand — we verify that, we don't assume it.

▸

Safe by default

Bounded, typed, invariant-checked APIs that are hard to misuse.

▸

Power-user bypasses

_unchecked fast paths for when you've already proven the invariant. You opt into the risk; you don't pay for it by default.

▸

Minimal checks, audited

Every check on the hot path is audited down to the absolute minimum. No redundant bounds checks, no defensive cruft.

▸

Validated in the assembly

We read the generated code for hot paths and confirm it's optimal — down to registers and branches.

▸

Unsafe is Miri-tested

The bypasses stay sound. Every unsafe path Miri can reach is covered.

Zero-allocation analytics

Welford moments, EMA, CUSUM, online regression, Kalman filters, change detection and control primitives across nexus-stats. Every type is O(1) per update with fixed memory.
